# Of the joy of a Good Conscience. I. The glory of a good man is the testimony of a good conscience. Keep a good conscience^ and thou shalt always have joy. A good conscience can bear very much, and is very joyful in the midst of adversity. A bad conscience is always fearful and uneasy. Sweetly wilt thou take thy rest, if thy heart reprehend thee not. Never rejoice but when thou hast done well. The wicked never have true joy, neither do they feel internal peace : because there is no peace to the tvicked, saith the Lord,'--^ Isaiah^ xlviii., 22. \
