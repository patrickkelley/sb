For before thou earnestly prayest to me, thou seekest in the mean time many comforts, and delightest thyself in outward things. And hence it comes to pass, that all things avail thee little, till thou take notice that I am He who delivers those that trust in me. Nor is there out of me any powerful help, nor profitable counsel, nor lasting remedy. But now, having recovered spirits after the storm, grow thou strong again in the light of my tender mercies : for 1 am at hand, to repair all, not only to the full, but even with abundance and above measure. II. Is anything difficult to me ? or shall I be like one that promises and does not perform ? Where is thy faith ? Stand firmly, and with perseverance. Have patience and be of good courage ; comfort will come to thee in its proper season. Wait for me, wait, I will come and cure thee. It is a temptation that troubles thee, and a vain fear that afrights thee. What does that solicitude about future accidents bring thee^ but only sorrow upon sorrow?
