X. Draw me away, and deliver me from all unstable comfort of creatures ; for no created thing can fully quiet and satisfy my desires. And the world also prophesies visions of peace to its yota* lies ; but that peace which it places in pleasure, in the satisfaction of pride and of all the passions, shows itself only at a distance, in order to mislead those 2a2 . who follow it, Bnd when they think themselves about to seize on it, it suddenly vanishes, as the dream of them that awake (Ps. Ixxii. 20). Real peace^ on the contrary, is but the tranquillity of a pure conscience ; it consists in restraining our desires, not in satisfying them. If there is a retired place, an obscure employment, a position, a rank contemptible to the world, it is especially there. The more the heart humiliates itself, the more it is calm and deep.
